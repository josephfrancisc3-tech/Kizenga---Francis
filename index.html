The Artist's Journey

Where Precision
Becomes Poetry

Francis Kizenga moves through the world with two languages: the clinical syntax of medicine and the silent grammar of graphite. Trained in both the anatomy of the human body and the discipline of pharmaceutical science, he arrived at fine art not as an escape from precision — but as its highest expression. Every portrait is, in essence, a diagnosis. A reading of light against shadow. Of presence against erasure.

"The pencil does not lie. Like a clinician's assessment, it reveals only what is truly there — and what we choose to see."

Working from his studio in Pwani, Tanzania, Francis has built a practice that treats portraiture as a form of care — an act of bearing witness. His charcoal works are distinguished by their tonal depth and psychological intimacy, while his graphite pieces carry the surgical exactness of someone who once mapped meridians of the human face in anatomy and now maps them again in the language of art.

Francis Kizenga — Pharmacist, Clinical Auditor, Fine Artist. Currently accepting commissions worldwide.

A commission with Francis is a considered collaboration — a slow and deliberate dialogue between subject, artist, and medium. From reference to final delivery, each work is treated with the same clinical attention Francis applies to every discipline he inhabits.

01
Initial Consultation
A brief exchange to discuss subject, format, and intention. References are reviewed; scope and size are confirmed.
02
Deposit & Proposal
A 50% deposit secures the commission. A written proposal confirming materials, dimensions, and timeline is issued.
03
Creation & Review
Progress images are shared at key stages. One round of revision is included prior to completion.
04
Delivery & Certificate
Final balance settled before dispatch. All originals ship with a certificate of authenticity and care instructions.
